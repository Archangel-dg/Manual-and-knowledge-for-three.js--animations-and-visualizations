Three.js Visual Quality Standard

Purpose

This document defines the visual requirements for every Three.js experience.

The objective is to create scenes that feel:

* premium
* cinematic
* intentional
* immersive
* professional

A technically correct scene is not automatically a high-quality scene.

Visual quality comes from the combination of:

* composition
* lighting
* materials
* camera
* atmosphere
* color
* detail
* motion

⸻

1. The Visual Hierarchy Principle

Every frame must have a clear hierarchy.

The viewer should immediately understand:

1. What is important?
2. Where should attention go?
3. What is interactive?
4. What is background?

Never allow every element to compete equally.

⸻

2. Composition Standards

Before adding detail, establish composition.

Evaluate:

* balance
* scale
* spacing
* contrast
* depth
* focal point
* negative space

⸻

Rule of Thirds

Important objects should rarely be placed randomly in the center.

Use:

* thirds
* diagonal composition
* leading lines
* visual balance

⸻

Depth Layers

Every scene should contain multiple depth levels.

Example:

Foreground:

Particles, UI elements, close objects

Midground:

Main subject

Background:

Environment, atmosphere, lighting

A flat scene feels artificial.

⸻

3. Camera Standards

The camera is the viewer’s eye.

A professional camera setup requires:

* intentional position
* intentional focal length
* controlled movement
* smooth transitions

⸻

Camera Distance

Too close:

* uncomfortable
* loses context

Too far:

* weak emotional connection

Find the balance between information and immersion.

⸻

Camera Movement

Camera movement should:

* reveal
* emphasize
* guide
* transition

Avoid movement without purpose.

⸻

4. Field of View Standards

The field of view changes perception.

Low FOV:

* luxury
* product visualization
* cinematic feeling

High FOV:

* action
* speed
* game environments

Choose based on experience.

⸻

5. Color Theory

Colors must support the experience.

Define:

Primary Color

The main identity.

Secondary Color

Supporting elements.

Accent Color

Attention and interaction.

Background Color

Atmosphere.

⸻

6. Contrast Management

Contrast controls attention.

Use:

* brightness contrast
* color contrast
* scale contrast
* motion contrast

The most important element should usually have the strongest contrast.

⸻

7. Material Quality Standards

Materials must communicate physical properties.

Every material requires consideration of:

* roughness
* reflectivity
* texture
* imperfections
* lighting response

⸻

Avoid

Pure colors.

Perfect surfaces.

Unlimited shine.

Unrealistic reflections.

⸻

Prefer

Subtle imperfections.

Realistic roughness.

Environment reflections.

Physical response.

⸻

8. Lighting Quality Standards

Lighting creates realism.

Every scene should define:

Key Light

Main illumination.

Fill Light

Controls shadows.

Rim Light

Separates objects from background.

Environment Light

Creates realistic reflections.

⸻

9. Cinematic Lighting Rules

Premium scenes often use:

* strong key direction
* soft shadows
* controlled highlights
* atmospheric depth
* color contrast

Avoid:

* equal brightness everywhere
* random point lights
* flat illumination

⸻

10. Atmosphere

Atmosphere creates scale.

Possible elements:

* fog
* particles
* dust
* volumetric light
* environmental effects

Atmosphere should support the scene.

Never add effects only because they look impressive.

⸻

11. Detail Distribution

Not every area needs maximum detail.

Professional scenes use:

High detail:

Main subject.

Medium detail:

Supporting elements.

Low detail:

Background.

This improves both quality and performance.

⸻

12. Realism Through Imperfection

Perfect objects feel artificial.

Add controlled imperfections:

* scratches
* uneven roughness
* small variations
* subtle movement
* environmental influence

Realism comes from controlled randomness.

⸻

13. Premium Product Visualization Standard

For luxury-style scenes:

Use:

* slow camera movement
* clean composition
* soft lighting
* high-quality materials
* minimal distractions

Reference feeling:

* premium product launch
* luxury advertisement
* cinematic reveal

⸻

14. Game Experience Standard

For interactive experiences:

Prioritize:

* readability
* feedback
* responsiveness
* clear focus

A beautiful game that confuses users has failed.

⸻

15. Visual Review Checklist

Before approving any scene:

Composition

✓ Clear focal point

✓ Balanced framing

✓ Good depth

Camera

✓ Intentional movement

✓ Correct perspective

✓ Comfortable viewing

Lighting

✓ Clear hierarchy

✓ Realistic shadows

✓ Good atmosphere

Materials

✓ Believable surfaces

✓ Correct reflections

✓ Physical properties

Overall

✓ Premium feeling

✓ Consistent style

✓ No unnecessary effects

⸻

Final Rule

Visual quality is not created by adding more elements.

Visual quality is created by making every element work together.
